# Yosemite Travel Guide

Date: 2026-03-17

Yosemite National Park is an American national park in California, United States, located in the western Sierra Nevada, straddling the drainage divide between Yosemite Valley (west) and the Tuolumne River watershed (east). Covering 748,542 acres, it is bordered on the southeast by Sequoia National Park, and the south by Sierra National Forest and Stanislaus National Forest. Designated a World Heritage Site in 1984, Yosemite receives nearly four million visitors each year. It contains nearly 95% of the park's 1,189 mi (1,914 km) of hiking trails, 800 established campsites, 17 miles (27 km) of paved roads, 6 miles (9.7 km) of bike paths, and 747 mi (1,202 km) of maintained roads.
